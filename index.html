Integrated Logistics and Energy Lab (ILEL)

The Integrated Logistics and Energy Lab (ILEL) develops advanced optimization and artificial intelligence methods for logistics systems operating under energy, sustainability, and infrastructure constraints. Our research integrates transport, energy systems, and uncertainty-aware decision-making, with a strong focus on real-world logistics applications.

About the Lab

ILEL sits at the logistics–energy interface: designing methods and decision support that account for grid congestion, charging infrastructure, sustainability targets, and uncertainty. We combine rigorous operations research with machine learning to enable scalable, deployable tools for transport planning and control.

Research Lines

Energy-Responsive Logistics Systems

  • Battery-electric truck routing under grid congestion
  • Charging infrastructure planning and control
  • Integrated logistics–energy planning
  • Hydrogen transport and distribution networks

Sustainable Transport Management

  • Electric and hydrogen vehicle routing
  • Reducing empty mileage and improving asset utilization
  • Robust and stochastic transport planning
  • Real-time transport control

Stochastic Optimization & AI

  • Markov Decision Processes and reinforcement learning
  • Stochastic and multi-stage optimization
  • Predict-then-optimize and recourse models
  • Hybrid OR–ML heuristics
